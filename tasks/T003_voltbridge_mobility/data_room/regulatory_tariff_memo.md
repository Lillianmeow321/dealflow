# Regulatory and Tariff Memo

Prepared for preliminary diligence.

## Europe Compliance

VoltBridge sells e-bikes in Europe under CE documentation and EN15194 compliance files. Current documentation covers the 2024 commuter and folding models. The 2025 cargo model and two battery pack variants require updated technical files before broad distribution.

Open items:

- Updated EN15194 test reports for the cargo model.
- Battery pack UN38.3 documentation for one supplier variant.
- Local-language manuals for Germany, France, and the Netherlands.
- Recycling and WEEE registration updates in two EU markets.
- Review of speed-assist firmware configuration for local rules.

## Tariff and Import Exposure

The current plan assumes no material tariff increase. However, counsel notes:

- EU anti-dumping scrutiny of certain China-origin e-bike components remains a monitoring item.
- Battery transport requirements may increase logistics cost by 1.2-1.8 percentage points of revenue.
- Some distributors are asking VoltBridge to absorb compliance-related logistics surcharges.

## Southeast Asia

Southeast Asia regulatory burden is lighter but fragmented. Thailand and Vietnam require local labeling and service documentation. Indonesia distribution would require additional product registration.

## Counsel View

Compliance risk is manageable but not fully reflected in the operating plan. The company should not ship the 2025 cargo model broadly in Europe until updated technical files are complete. Counsel recommends a dedicated compliance budget and stronger documentation control before aggressive 2026 European expansion.
