# Distributor Contracts Summary

## European Distributors

VoltBridge's European revenue is concentrated in three large distributors.

| Distributor | 2025 revenue | Share of Europe revenue | Payment terms | Contract notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| NordBike GmbH | $10.6M | 29.3% | 90 days | Exclusive for Germany/Austria commuter line |
| VeloMaison SARL | $7.8M | 21.6% | 75 days | France/Benelux; rebate if annual volume target met |
| Nordic Motion AB | $5.7M | 15.7% | 90 days | Scandinavia; requested more warranty support |
| Other distributors | $12.1M | 33.4% | 45-60 days | Smaller accounts |

Top three distributors represent 66.6% of Europe revenue and 51.5% of total company revenue.

## Contract Issues

- NordBike has requested extended payment terms for the 2026 season.
- VeloMaison's contract includes a rebate if sell-through targets are not reached.
- Nordic Motion requested partial reimbursement for motor controller failures in winter conditions.
- Two potential new European distributors require local spare parts stock before signing.

## Southeast Asia Retail Partners

Southeast Asia has lower customer concentration but weaker pricing power. Retail partners typically require in-store demo units, marketing allowances, and local service commitments. Payment terms range from 45 to 75 days.

## Management View

Management believes distributor concentration is acceptable because top partners provide scale and local service coverage. They argue that expanding two new European distributors in 2026 will reduce concentration.
