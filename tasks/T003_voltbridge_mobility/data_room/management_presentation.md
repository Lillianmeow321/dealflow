# VoltBridge Mobility Management Presentation Summary

## Company

VoltBridge Mobility designs, sources, and sells e-bikes for overseas markets. The company is headquartered in Shenzhen and works with contract manufacturers in Guangdong and Zhejiang. It sells through European distributors, Amazon marketplaces, a small DTC site, and Southeast Asian retail partners.

## Management's Key Claims

- 2025 revenue reached $46.8M, up 51% year over year.
- Europe remains the core profit pool, while Southeast Asia is the fastest-growing region.
- Blended gross margin is reported at 28.4%.
- Management reports positive adjusted EBITDA of $1.1M after excluding launch marketing, incremental warranty accrual, and one-time compliance expenses.
- Management reports cash conversion cycle of 95 days and says working capital will normalize after 2026 distributor onboarding.
- Management reports inventory days at 118 days and says this is normal for seasonal e-bike sales.
- Warranty cost is reported at 3.2% of revenue.
- The company plans to grow revenue to $72M in 2026 by expanding Southeast Asia retail partners and adding two new European distributors.
- Management says regulatory exposure is manageable because key models already have CE and EN15194 documentation.

## Product Lines

1. Urban commuter e-bikes.
2. Folding e-bikes for apartment dwellers and commuters.
3. Cargo e-bikes for families and last-mile delivery.
4. Entry-level Southeast Asia models with smaller batteries and lower ASP.

## Channel Strategy

Management plans to:

- Maintain Europe distributors as the premium channel.
- Grow Amazon and DTC to improve direct customer data.
- Expand Southeast Asia retail to increase volume.
- Build a small localized service network in Germany, France, Thailand, and Vietnam.

## Capital Use

The company is raising $20M growth capital to fund:

- Inventory for 2026 selling season.
- European service centers and spare parts stock.
- Southeast Asia retail expansion.
- Battery certification and compliance testing.
- Working capital for distributor payment terms.

## Management's Recommendation

Management recommends investors focus on revenue growth, global e-bike adoption, supply-chain advantage, and channel expansion. They describe current inventory and warranty metrics as normal growing pains for a seasonal hardware exporter.
