# Copyright and IP Risk Memo

Prepared by outside counsel for preliminary diligence.

## Summary

StoryForge has implemented a basic content review process, but its IP controls are still early. The company does not currently train foundation models. It uses third-party image and video generation tools, internal prompt libraries, human editing, and licensed stock elements.

## Current Controls

- Writers create original story outlines and character bibles.
- Editors are instructed not to reference living artists by name in prompts.
- Final episodes go through human review before publication.
- The team logs prompt templates for top series.
- Music is sourced from licensed libraries.

## Open Issues

1. Asset provenance is not consistently tracked at the panel level.
2. Some concept art prompts use genre references that may produce style similarity to well-known manga/anime franchises.
3. The team has received two platform notices requesting clarification on character similarity.
4. No formal chain-of-title process exists for AI-generated visual assets.
5. The company has not yet run external clearance for its top three series.
6. Contracts with freelance story editors do not consistently assign derivative work rights.

## Counsel View

The risk is manageable for testing and early distribution, but not yet appropriate for large-scale IP licensing or adaptation without stronger provenance tracking, clearance review, and contributor contracts.

Counsel recommends:

- Panel-level asset provenance logging.
- Formal clearance review for top series.
- Updated contractor IP assignment templates.
- Avoiding prompt references to identifiable franchises, artists, or protected character styles.
