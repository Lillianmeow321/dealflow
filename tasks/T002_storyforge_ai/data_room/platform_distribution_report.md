# Platform Distribution Report

## Distribution Mix

StoryForge distributes short-form episodes across TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, Webtoon-style communities, and the StoryForge app.

Last 90 days:

| Platform | Share of total views | Share of engaged viewers | Share of paid users | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| TikTok | 71% | 68% | 63% | Primary discovery channel; strong hook testing |
| YouTube Shorts | 18% | 20% | 15% | Lower paid conversion but more stable long-tail views |
| Instagram Reels | 6% | 5% | 3% | Weak conversion |
| Webtoon-style communities | 3% | 4% | 8% | Smaller but higher-intent users |
| StoryForge app direct | 2% | 3% | 11% | Highest monetization per user but small funnel |

## Algorithm Exposure

TikTok remains the largest acquisition source. In March, a recommendation change reduced average new-series first-week impressions by 37%. The team offset some of the decline by testing more hooks and moving top-performing series into paid app retargeting.

## Paid Acquisition

The company has begun paid app acquisition tests. Early campaigns show:

- Average CPI: $1.42
- Trial-to-paid conversion: 6.5%
- Day-7 app retention after paid acquisition: 18%
- Payback period: not yet proven

## Distribution Team View

The team believes platform volatility is manageable because AI production allows more rapid testing. However, the current funnel still depends heavily on TikTok for discovery. The StoryForge app is strategically important but too small to prove owned-distribution durability.
