# Manufacturing Plan

HomePal plans to launch HomePal One through a Shenzhen-based contract manufacturer with prior experience in smart speakers and small mobility devices. The current plan is to begin pilot production in July 2026 and mass production in September 2026.

## Pilot Build Results

The latest engineering validation build produced 600 units.

- First-pass yield: 86%
- Final yield after rework: 93%
- Main defect categories:
  - Motor calibration drift: 5.5% of units
  - Camera module alignment issue: 3.2% of units
  - Battery housing tolerance issue: 2.7% of units
  - Speaker vibration at high volume: 1.8% of units
- Average rework cost: $47 per affected unit

## Management Mass Production Plan

Management assumes the following ramp:

- Month 1: 2,000 units, 88% first-pass yield
- Month 2: 4,000 units, 91% first-pass yield
- Month 3: 6,000 units, 93% first-pass yield
- Month 4 onward: 8,000 units per month, 95% first-pass yield

Management expects most yield improvement to come from final tooling, assembly fixtures, and supplier process control.

## Open Manufacturing Items

- Final enclosure tooling is not complete.
- Battery certification is in progress and expected by August 2026.
- Drop test and long-duration motor reliability testing have not completed.
- The contract manufacturer has not previously produced a mobile home robot at this level of mechanical complexity.
- Management has not finalized spare parts policy or repair turnaround time.

## Launch Timing Risk

The launch plan assumes certification, tooling, reliability testing, and supplier readiness all complete before September 2026. A one-month delay in battery certification or enclosure tooling would push the first shipment window into Q4 2026.
