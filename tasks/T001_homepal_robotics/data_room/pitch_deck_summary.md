# HomePal Robotics Pitch Deck Summary

## Company

HomePal Robotics builds AI-powered companion robots for households. The robot combines voice interaction, computer vision, lightweight mobility, reminders, and a companion personality layer. The first product, HomePal One, is positioned as a family assistant for daily routines, elderly care reminders, and child interaction.

## Management's Key Claims

- HomePal has completed 243 structured household pilot placements across Shanghai, Shenzhen, Hangzhou, Singapore, and Taipei.
- Demo-to-preorder conversion is reported at 34% across offline demo events.
- Management reports 338 gross preorders and $101,400 of preorder deposits.
- Management reports an average of 6.8 daily interaction sessions per active household.
- The company reports 72% 30-day active usage retention in its "core care cohorts."
- Management expects a $1,299 launch price and 38% hardware gross margin at mass production.
- The Series A plan targets 25,000 units shipped in the first 12 months after launch.
- HomePal plans to outsource final assembly to a Shenzhen contract manufacturer and reach 95% manufacturing yield by month 4 of mass production.

## Product Positioning

Management describes three core use cases:

1. Elderly care reminders: medication reminders, hydration reminders, simple check-ins, family notification if reminders are ignored.
2. Child interaction: bedtime stories, homework encouragement, language practice, interactive games.
3. Family assistant: calendar reminders, shopping list capture, voice notes, and household routine prompts.

## Business Model

HomePal plans to sell hardware at launch and add a $14.99/month premium subscription for advanced memory, personalized routines, and family notification features. The current structured pilot does not charge subscription fees for the base experience, although 58 households have paid for extended pilot access or premium feature trials.

Management states that preorder deposits validate willingness to pay, but the company has not yet recognized material recurring subscription revenue.

## AI Stack

The product uses:

- Third-party large language model APIs for open-ended conversation.
- A proprietary household routine memory layer.
- On-device wake-word detection and basic navigation.
- Cloud-based vision classification for object and scene recognition.

Management states that the long-term differentiation will come from household context memory, robot personality tuning, and multimodal interaction design rather than owning a foundation model.

## Current Fundraise

HomePal is raising an $18M Series A to fund:

- Tooling and manufacturing deposits.
- Hardware certification and reliability testing.
- Hiring for embedded systems, robotics QA, and family care product design.
- Launch inventory for the first 10,000 units.

## Management's Recommendation

Management argues that HomePal has crossed from "interesting demo" to "repeatable household behavior" and is ready for launch. They recommend that investors focus on preorders, active-household engagement, and the size of the family care market.
