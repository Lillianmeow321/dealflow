# User Interview Notes

The product team conducted 24 semi-structured interviews with pilot households after 30 days. Notes below are anonymized and grouped by use case.

## Elderly Care Households

- 8 of 10 elderly care households said the robot was most useful for medication reminders, hydration reminders, and low-friction check-ins.
- 7 of 10 said they would consider paying for a family notification subscription if reminders were reliable.
- 3 households said the robot reduced the need for daily reminder calls from adult children.
- 2 households reported missed reminders when home Wi-Fi was unstable.
- 1 household returned the unit because the elderly parent found the robot's movement distracting at night.

Representative quotes:

- "My mother responds to the robot more consistently than to phone alarms because it feels less clinical."
- "The reminder feature is useful, but I need the family notification to be dependable before I pay monthly."
- "It is not doing care work, but it gives us a little more confidence during the day."

## Child Interaction Households

- 6 of 9 child interaction households said children were excited during the first week.
- 5 of 9 said engagement dropped after the second or third week once the story and game patterns felt repetitive.
- 4 of 9 raised concerns about the robot encouraging too much screen-like interaction.
- 3 of 9 submitted support tickets about inappropriate or confusing answers during open-ended conversation.
- 2 of 9 said they would only keep the robot if parental controls improved.

Representative quotes:

- "It was magical for three days. By week three, my son mostly asked it silly questions."
- "The stories repeat similar arcs. It needs better age control and more structured learning modes."
- "I do not want a robot improvising answers to my child without stronger guardrails."

## Family Assistant Households

- 3 of 5 family assistant households used reminders and voice notes at least four times per week.
- 2 of 5 said a smart speaker already handled most of these needs.
- 4 of 5 said they would not buy the robot only for calendar, shopping list, and reminder functionality.

Representative quotes:

- "It is more charming than a smart speaker, but not enough better for the price."
- "The robot is fun, but for family organization I would rather use the apps we already have."

## Product Team Summary

The clearest pull appears to be elderly care reminders plus family notification. Child interaction generates the strongest initial excitement but also the most complaints and fastest novelty decay. Family assistant use cases are convenient but not yet differentiated from existing smart speakers or mobile apps.
